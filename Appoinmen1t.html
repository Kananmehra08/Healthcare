Trends in Elderly Care Technology for 2024...




Remote monitoring



Devices such as sensors and webcams can help caregivers monitor the elderly without being physically present. Sensors can detect movement and alert caregivers if the elderly fall.




Telehealth



Virtual care services allow seniors to access medical professionals without visiting a clinic. This can reduce travel costs and provide more frequent check-ins.




Wearable Health Technology



Fitness trackers and smartwatches can track vital signs, sleep patterns, and exercise.




Phone applications



Apps can help the elderly monitor their medication, heart rate, and location. They can also help families and caregivers share data with doctors.




Robot assistants



Robots can help the elderly by reminding them to take prescriptions, exploring digital content, and communicating with relatives




Intelligent home monitoring system



These systems can help monitor the elderly and detect falls or near falls.
